The Problem

Every helmet on the market is fighting the wrong battle.

Current protective headgear - from certified football helmets to premium cycling lids - is designed around a single principle: absorb the impact. Crush the foam. Pass the test. Every innovation in the space for the last thirty years has been a variation on that same passive model.

The problem is that absorption is not enough. The force that most often destroys a brain is not the linear hit. It is the rotation that follows.

87%
of concussions and traumatic brain injuries are caused by rotational head acceleration - not linear impact. The brain twists relative to the skull. Axons shear. Damage accumulates across a career, a season, a childhood. Every helmet on the market addresses the other 13%.
🧠
Passive-only protection
Foam, gel, and sliding layers absorb energy after the fact. They generate zero counter-force against the rotation that causes injury.
⚡
No active response
No current helmet can detect an incoming rotational event, compute a response, and act on it in real time. The technology did not exist in protective equipment - until now.
📋
Certifications miss the mark
NOCSAE and CPSC standards still center on linear peak-g thresholds. The regulatory landscape is beginning to shift - and Inercept is ahead of it.
The Solution

Active. Real-time. Counter-force.

Inercept is the first wearable protective equipment technology that actively generates counter-force opposing rotational head acceleration at the moment of impact.

The system detects, computes, and responds - engaging faster than any passive material can react, and faster than the biological consequences of rotation begin.

Active counter-torque generation Inercept generates real-time force opposing the angular acceleration of the head. It does not absorb - it opposes.
Sub-millisecond engagement The system engages within a small fraction of the total impact duration - before the brain has meaningfully moved relative to the skull.
Wearable. Embedded. Invisible. Designed to integrate within existing helmet form factors. The Inercept technology lives inside - the helmet OEM's brand lives outside.
Multi-domain architecture Scoped to wearable protective equipment broadly - not headgear alone. Sports, military, industrial, aerospace, and medical rehabilitation applications.
"Passive helmets absorb the hit. Inercept absorbs nothing - it opposes the force that causes injury. That is a categorically different kind of protection."
